A 2012 DVRPC report entitled, The Delaware Valley Freight Center Inventory: Taking Stock of a Vital Regional Asset, applied a traditional planning approach of identifying and stratifying centers (e.g., as used in the DVRPC long-range plan, Connections) and designated major concentrations of freight transportation activity in the region. Freight Centers are defined as contiguous land parcels (under either joint or individual ownership) that are dedicated to one of the following types of freight activity: light manufacturing, heavy manufacturing, distribution, transportation, quarry/mining, and utility.

The Freight Centers were identified using GIS data, field visits, stakeholder input, and Internet research. A minimum size of 250 acres was established, with allowances for smaller Freight Centers or new categories to be created at a future date. Factors such as shared infrastructure and commodities handled were considered when drawing up Freight Center boundaries.

The Freight Centers were placed into one of three categories, based primarily on acreage, with one slight adjustment made based on employment data. The breakdown of the region’s 44 identified Freight Centers and the definition of the three categories are:

  • 8 Mega Freight Centers
    • 1,500 or more acres, or 700+ acres and over 3,000 manufacturing jobs
  • 13 Major Freight Centers
    • 700-1,499 acres, or 250+ acres and over 3,000 manufacturing jobs
  • 23 Intermediate Freight Centers
    • 250-699 acres, or under 250 acres and over 3,000 manufacturing jobs

Mega Freight Centers are the largest size of identified centers with at least 1,500 acres of freight-related land use or over 3,000 manufacturing jobs. The concentration of land within the Mega Freight Centers tends to be pronounced, in large part because several of them are owned by a single firm or industrial real estate developer. Of note, all eight of the region’s Mega Freight Centers are served, or have the possibility of being served, by at least three different modes of transportation.

The region’s eight identified Mega Freight Centers are:

  • Falls/Keystone Industrial Port Complex
  • Schuylkill River West/Philadelphia International Airport
  • Logan/Pureland
  • Northeast Philadelphia Airport/Byberry Road
  • Schuylkill River East/Girard Point
  • Marcus Hook/Trainer
  • Burlington Township/Haines Industrial Center
  • Chester/Eddystone

Major Freight Centers range in size from 700 to 1,499 acres of freight-related land use, or they combine 250-700 acres with over 3,000 manufacturing jobs. Seven of the nine counties in the DVRPC region have a Major Freight Center and six of them are located along the Delaware River.

The region’s 13 identified Major Freight Centers are:

  • Paulsboro/Greenwich
  • South Philadelphia/Packer Avenue
  • Warminster/Northampton
  • Pennsauken/NJ 90
  • West Deptford/Mantua Grove Road
  • Robbinsville/NJ Turnpike Interchange 7A
  • Downingtown/Boot Road
  • Falls/Penn-Warner
  • Bensalem/Street Road
  • Camden/Gloucester City
  • Pottstown/Limerick
  • Upper Gwynedd/West Point
  • Towamencin/Hatfield

Intermediate Freight Centers consist of 250 to 700 acres of freight-related land use, or less than 250 acres with over 3,000 manufacturing jobs. While Mega and Major Freight Centers tend to be more tightly bunched, several of the Intermediate Freight Centers are more spread out. Most Intermediate Freight Centers fall into two patterns of development: a series of independent industrial parks; or a cluster dominated by a large manufacturing facility.

The Intermediate Freight Centers are the most numerous of the three center designations (i.e., 23 in total) and offer considerable geographic diversity. Intermediate Freight Centers are spread relatively equally throughout the region, instead of being concentrated solely along the Delaware River.
